    The 20-Day Clock

    You must send this notice within 20 days of *starting* work. If you miss the deadline, you generally forfeit lien rights for any work done prior to 20 days ago.

  • 2

    It's Not a Lien

    Sending this notice is standard procedure. It is NOT an accusation of non-payment; it merely reserves your right to file a lien later if needed.

    Certified Mail Required

    Regular mail isn't enough. We use USPS Certified Mail to provide the tracking number you need for the Affidavit of Service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Tracking Number enough?

Yes. For a Preliminary Notice, the legal requirement is generally to prove you *sent* it via Certified Mail. Our system generates the official USPS Tracking Number you need for your Affidavit of Service.

Do you offer refunds?

If our system fails to mail your letter, we issue a 100% automatic refund. However, once the letter is handed to USPS (postage paid), we cannot offer refunds for user errors (like typos) or delivery refusals.

How fast is it sent?

We process orders instantly. Orders placed before 10 AM PST typically enter the USPS mail stream the same business day. You receive the tracking number immediately on screen.

Why isn't this free?

We save you the trip to the post office, the cost of Certified Mail envelopes (~$7), ink, and printing. Your time is worth more than the difference.
