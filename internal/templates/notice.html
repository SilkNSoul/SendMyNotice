NOTICE TO PROPERTY OWNER

EVEN THOUGH YOU HAVE PAID YOUR CONTRACTOR IN FULL, if the person or firm that has given you this notice is not paid in full for labor, service, equipment, or material provided or to be provided to your construction project, a lien may be placed on your property. Foreclosure of the lien may lead to loss of all or part of your property. You may wish to protect yourself against this consequence by (1) requiring your contractor to furnish a signed release by the person or firm that has given you this notice before making payment to your contractor or (2) any other method for protection that is appropriate under the circumstances.

This notice is required by law to be served by the undersigned as a statement of your legal rights. This notice is not intended to reflect upon the financial condition of the contractor or the person employed by you on the construction project.

If you record a notice of cessation or completion of your construction project, you must within 10 days after recording, send a copy of the notice of cessation or completion to your contractor and the person or firm that has given you this notice. The failure to send you a notice of cessation or completion will extend the deadline to record a claim of lien. You are not required to send these notices unless you are a residential homeowner of a dwelling containing four or fewer units.
